Privacy Policy

How Alfa Express collects, uses, and protects your personal data when you use this website, in line with the GDPR and Polish data-protection law.

Last updated: 5 July 2026

1. Who we are

The data controller responsible for your personal data is Alfa Express Sp. z o.o., registered office at Głogoczów 944/1, 32-444 Głogoczów, Poland, entered in the Register of Entrepreneurs of the National Court Register (KRS) under number 0000914093, NIP 9492249163, REGON 389560996 ("Alfa Express", "we", "us", "our"). Our office is at Bosaków 5A, 31-476 Kraków.

For any question about this policy or about how we handle your data, contact us at central.alfa.express@gmail.com or by post at the address above.

2. Do we have a Data Protection Officer?

Under Article 37 GDPR, a Data Protection Officer (DPO) is mandatory only for public authorities, or where an organisation's core activities involve large-scale, regular and systematic monitoring of individuals, or large-scale processing of special categories of data. Based on how we currently process data — responding to enquiries and handling job applications — we do not meet these thresholds, so we have not formally designated a DPO. Questions about your data should instead be sent to the contact address in Section 1. We keep this assessment under review as our activities develop.

3. What personal data we collect

We collect the following categories of personal data:

  • Contact-form enquiries: your name, email address, phone number, the subject you select, and the content of your message.
  • Job applications: your name, contact details, and the contents of your CV or message, which may include work history, education, licences and certificates (for example a C+E driving licence, Driver CPC card, or ADR certificate) if you choose to include them.
  • Technical data: IP address, browser and device type, and general usage data generated automatically as you browse the site or interact with embedded third-party content such as fonts or maps.
  • Client and shipment data: if you become a client, information exchanged in the course of arranging and providing transport, warehousing, or customs services, handled under the terms of our service agreement with you.

4. Why we use your data, and our legal basis

  • Responding to your enquiry: based on your consent when you tick the consent box on our contact form (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR) and, where your enquiry concerns a possible service, as steps taken at your request before entering into a contract (Article 6(1)(b)).
  • Providing our logistics services: performance of a contract with you or your organisation (Article 6(1)(b)).
  • Assessing your job application: steps taken at your request before entering into an employment contract (Article 6(1)(b)), together with Article 22¹ of the Polish Labour Code, which sets out the categories of data an employer may request from a candidate. Any further information you choose to include in your CV is processed on the basis of your consent (Article 6(1)(a)).
  • Keeping your CV for future vacancies: only with your separate, explicit consent (Article 6(1)(a)) — see Section 7.
  • Operating and securing the website: our legitimate interest in keeping the site functional, secure, and free of spam or abuse (Article 6(1)(f)).
  • Meeting legal obligations: for example accounting, tax, and transport-documentation requirements (Article 6(1)(c)).

5. Cookies and similar technologies

This website does not itself set analytics or advertising cookies. Several pages do load resources from third-party providers, which can process technical data — including your IP address — when the resource loads, and may set their own cookies:

  • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com / fonts.gstatic.com) — used to display our typefaces.
  • Google Maps — embedded on our Home and Contact pages to show our locations.
  • Font Awesome (cdnjs.cloudflare.com) — used for icons.
  • AOS and Swiper (unpkg.com / jsdelivr.net) — used for on-page animation and image carousels.
  • Web3Forms (web3forms.com) — our contact-form provider, which receives what you submit through the form in order to deliver it to us.

We also store your language selection (EN/UK/PL) locally in your browser so the site remembers your preference; this is strictly necessary to provide a feature you have chosen and does not require consent.

Where a technology is not strictly necessary to deliver a feature you have requested, Polish and EU law (Article 155 of the Electronic Communications Law, implementing the ePrivacy Directive) requires us to ask for your consent before it loads, through a cookie/consent notice on the site, and to let you withdraw that consent as easily as you gave it. You can also block or delete cookies at any time through your browser settings, though this may affect how some pages display.

Some of the providers above are based outside the European Economic Area (for example in the United States). Where that is the case, the transfer currently relies on the recipient's certification under the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework adequacy decision or, where that does not apply, on Standard Contractual Clauses.

6. Who we share your data with

We share personal data only where necessary, with:

  • Our staff who need it to respond to you, process an application, or deliver a service.
  • IT, hosting, and website service providers, including those listed in Section 5.
  • Professional advisers such as our accountant or lawyer, where necessary.
  • Public authorities, where we are required by law to disclose information (for example tax, customs, or transport regulators).

We do not sell personal data, and we do not share it with third parties for their own independent marketing purposes.

7. How long we keep your data

  • General enquiries that do not lead to a business relationship: up to 12 months from your last contact with us, unless a longer period is needed to establish, exercise, or defend a legal claim.
  • Client and contract data: for the duration of our business relationship, plus the periods required by Polish accounting law (generally five years from the relevant financial year-end) and by the statute of limitations for related claims (generally three years for claims connected with business activity, six years otherwise).
  • Job applications for a specific role: for the duration of that recruitment process, plus up to three months afterwards, then deleted — unless you give us separate, explicit consent to keep your CV on file for future vacancies, in which case we keep it for up to 12 months from your consent, renewable if you confirm you would like us to continue.
  • Website technical data: kept only briefly, for the period needed for security and troubleshooting.

These periods are our current internal guidance and may be refined as we formalise our records of processing under Article 30 GDPR.

8. Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the right to: access the personal data we hold about you; request correction of inaccurate data; request erasure of your data; request that we restrict processing; receive your data in a portable format; object to processing based on our legitimate interest; and withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before you withdrew it.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Polish supervisory authority: Prezes Urzędu Ochrony Danych Osobowych (President of the Personal Data Protection Office, UODO), ul. Stawki 2, 00-193 Warszawa, uodo.gov.pl, or with the supervisory authority in your own EU member state.

We do not carry out automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects you.

9. Do you have to give us your data?

Providing your data is voluntary, but it is necessary for us to respond to your enquiry, process your application, or provide our services. Fields marked as required on our forms are the minimum we need for that purpose; if you do not provide them, we may not be able to help.

10. Children's privacy

This website and our services are directed at businesses and individuals aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time, for example if our data practices or the applicable law change. The "last updated" date at the top will show when it was last revised — please check back periodically.

12. Contact us

If you have questions about this policy or how we handle your data, contact us at central.alfa.express@gmail.com, by phone at +48 571 975 922, or by post at Głogoczów 944/1, 32-444 Głogoczów, Polska.
